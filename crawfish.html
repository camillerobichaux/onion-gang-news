Crawfish Shortage Prompts Panic Buying at Opelousas Market

By Remy Hebert | June 27, 2025

Local seafood vendors were overwhelmed Friday morning as residents rushed to secure what little crawfish remained in stock. The panic was sparked by rumors of a regional shortage fueled by unseasonably low water levels and a mysterious algae bloom affecting several Acadiana ponds.

“We had folks lined up before we even opened. One guy offered to pay triple for a 10-pound sack,” said Danielle Thibodeaux, owner of Thib’s Boil Stop. “It was worse than Black Friday.”

The Louisiana Department of Wildlife and Fisheries issued a statement urging calm, but did not confirm whether the shortage would impact the upcoming Fourth of July weekend.

Local meteorologist Gary Fontenot noted, “The drought conditions are real, but the algae reports haven’t been verified. Folks just need to simmer down and let the season do its thing.”

Meanwhile, black market Facebook groups have reportedly seen a surge in crawfish barter posts. One user offered two sacks in exchange for a gently used riding mower.
