Why Are All the Town's Squirrels Missing?

By Casey Dugas | June 22, 2025

Residents across Opelousas have noticed a strange silence in the trees — the squirrels are gone. Normally a staple of backyard scenes and park life, the furry acrobats seem to have vanished without a trace.

“We used to have six or seven squirrels fighting over our pecan tree every morning. Haven’t seen a one in over a week,” said homeowner Blaine Richard. “It’s spooky.”

Speculation is rampant. Some blame a surge in hawk activity, others whisper about environmental toxins or even supernatural forces. One local teen posted a TikTok suggesting the squirrels are staging a coordinated migration to Ville Platte.

The Louisiana Department of Wildlife says no official study is underway, but encourages anyone with sightings (or suspicious lack thereof) to call their regional office.

“Sometimes nature just moves on,” said wildlife officer Mandy Dupre. “Sometimes it takes the squirrels with it.”
